Section - used for grouping together thematically related content

The footer element represents a footer for its nearest ancestor sectioning content or sectioning root element

aside - tangentially related to the content surrounding it

  • When used within an article element, the aside content should be related to that particular article it is containedd within
  • When used outside on article element, the aside contents should be specifically related to the site
  • An aside element is appropriate when it is used to represent content that is not the primary focus of an article or page, but it is still related to the article or page

The nav element represents a section of a page that links to other pages or to parts within the page: a section with navigation links

The nav element is intended for "major navigation"

The Article element - represents a complete, or self-contained, composition in document, page, application, or site and that is, in principle, independently distributable or reusable, e.g. in syndication

The article element is another type of section. It is used for self-contained related content

Some uses for the article tag

  • a blog post
  • a news story
  • a comment on a post
  • a review

The main element represents the main content of the body of a document or application

consists of content that directly related to or expands upon the central topic of a document or central functionality of an application

  1. do not include more than one main element in the document
  2. do not include the main element inside of an article,aside, footer, header, or nav element
